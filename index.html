The "Why": Hormone Physiology (hCG)

Home pregnancy kits detect human Chorionic Gonadotropin (hCG), a hormone secreted by cells surrounding the developing embryo after implantation.

Implantation Window (Days 9-11 post-ovulation):

hCG starts appearing in bloodstream but remains under typical test detection thresholds (< 5 mIU/mL).

Early Detection Threshold (Days 12-14 post-ovulation):

hCG climbs to 10-25 mIU/mL. Ultra-sensitive tests might catch a faint positive, but false negative risks are high.

Missed Period & Beyond (Day 15+ post-ovulation):

hCG levels double every 48 hours, scaling over 50-100 mIU/mL. General home testing kits achieve 99% accuracy.

The "How": Step-by-Step Instructions

  • 1
    Collect First Morning Urine Hormone levels are most concentrated after sleeping, reducing the risk of water-diluted negative readings.
  • 2
    Respect timing guidelines Submerge or drip urine per package instructions. Check the result window strictly within the 3-5 minute timeframe. Ignore lines that appear after 10 minutes (evaporation lines).
  • 3
    Understanding Faint Lines Even an extremely faint colored line in the test window represents a positive detection of hCG. Evaporation lines are typically colorless or grey shadow lines.
  • 4
    Confirmation Steps If you get a positive test, or get a negative test but your period is still delayed, wait 48 hours and re-test, or contact a healthcare provider for a quantitative serum hCG blood test.
